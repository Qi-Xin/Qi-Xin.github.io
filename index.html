RESEARCH EXPERIENCES




• Neurostatistics Group
      Carnegie Mellon University       Advisor: Prof. Robert Kass            Jul 2017 – Sep 2017

  • Explored causes and provided solutions for the divergence problem: statistical models of single neuron (GLMs) pass goodness-of-fit test but show unphysiological explosion firing rate in the simulation.

  • Proposed a new framework that prevent inherent divergence from the original model.

• Laboratory of Computational Neuroscience of Small Animals
      USTC       Advisor: Prof. Wen Quan            Jan 2017 – Present

  • Created a realistic neural network model that can explain and reproduce the behavioral statistics of C. elegans escape response.
  • The model correctly predicted the outcomes of multiple experiments, including behavioral changes under various optogenetic manipulations, genetic analyses and calcium imaging.
  • Analytically calculated dynamics of the neural network model and interpreted behavior statistics from the view of statistical physics.
  • Trained a recurrent network exhibiting the escape response with evolutionary algorithm, which matches the proposed model undesignedly.
